# 📊 Customer Churn Insights

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Overall Churn Behavior
The analysis shows a significant portion of customers are churning, indicating retention is a critical business issue.

---

### 2. Contract Type Impact
Month-to-month customers have the highest churn rate compared to longer-term contracts. 
This suggests that short commitment plans are strongly associated with customer attrition.

---

### 3. Tenure is a Strong Predictor
Customers with low tenure (especially 0–6 months) show the highest churn probability. 
This indicates that early customer experience is crucial for retention.

---

### 4. Internet Service Influence
Customers using certain internet services (especially Fiber Optic) tend to churn more compared to others,
suggesting possible service quality or pricing concerns.

---

### 5. Online Security Effect
Customers without online security features are significantly more likely to churn, 
showing that add-on services improve retention.

---

### 6. High-Value Customers Are Not Always Stable
Customers with higher monthly charges show mixed churn behavior, 
indicating that price sensitivity may still play a role even in high-value segments.

---

### 7. Risk Segmentation Insight
The risk scoring model identifies a clear High Risk segment dominated by:
- Short tenure customers
- Month-to-month contracts
- Missing security services

---

### 8. Customer Lifetime Value Insight
Retained customers show significantly higher lifetime value compared to churned customers,
reinforcing the importance of retention strategies.

---

### 9. Churn Drivers Are Combined Factors
Churn is not driven by a single feature. The highest risk comes from combinations such as:
- Month-to-month contracts + Fiber optic internet + No online security

---

### 10. Long-Term Customers Are Stable
Customers with higher tenure demonstrate strong retention, suggesting that loyalty increases significantly over time.

---

## 💡 Business Conclusion

The strongest drivers of churn are:
- Contract type (short-term agreements)
- Early-stage customer lifecycle
- Lack of security add-ons
- Specific service combinations

To reduce churn, the business should focus on:
- Improving early onboarding experience
- Encouraging long-term contracts
- Bundling security services
- Targeting high-risk customer segments proactively
